The Skills to Build Your Big Idea

Innovation in the Heart of Ecuador

Join us for a full-day workshop at Yachay City of Knowledge, and learn Stanford University's winning strategies for creating products and services that matter. Instructors from Stanford's own d.school classes will lead participants through the process.

Entrepreneurs & Students Welcome

Do you have a few ideas already? Not sure where to start? Our intensive program is designed to teach you the skills you need and help you build a network of other local innovators.

Crucial Skills, Hands-on Experience

Move through the entire design lifecycle of a product, from user interviews to building and testing a prototype. Learn hireable skills by doing.

Innovation is about meeting the challenges that we encounter in our lives.

These workshops will focus on some of the local challenges facing The City of Knowledge. You'll work on real problems, and you'll master real skills. Best of all, you're helping Yachay's developing community.

Workshop Instruction


Caitria O'Neill

Design strategist and innovation coach, and former fellow and adjunct faculty at Stanford’s Hasso Plattner Institute of Design (d.school). Founder and CEO of Recovers.org, internationally recognized disaster relief technology company. Design work & training for Lean Startup, Hyatt, Airbnb, Boys & Girls Club, Outset Medical. Work profiled by TED.com, FastCompany, and Forbes among other publications. Recipient of a US White House Champion of Change award for contributions to design in the field of resilience and disaster recovery. Graduate of Harvard University.
